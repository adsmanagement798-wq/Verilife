FAQ

Questions we actually get.

How is VeriLife different from ChatGPT or Claude with web search?
▼
General-purpose LLMs with web search bolt-ons still rely on the model to synthesize retrieved content — and the model will still confabulate when uncertain. VeriLife's architecture inverts this: the verification layer is the primary component, and the language model is downstream of it. Claims are verified before they're assembled into a response, not after. The result is a system that fails gracefully instead of confidently fabricating.
What does "zero hallucination policy" mean in practice?
▼
It means VeriLife's response pipeline is designed to surface uncertainty rather than hide it. When a claim cannot be verified against live sources, it is flagged as unverified in the response metadata rather than presented as fact. You'll never get a confident wrong answer — you'll get a low-confidence flagged answer, which you can choose to investigate further.
Can I use VeriLife in a regulated industry like healthcare or finance?
▼
Yes. The Enterprise tier includes a HIPAA-compliant and SOC 2 mode with full audit trail logging, on-premises deployment options, and a dedicated compliance engineer. Many of our production customers are in legal intelligence, financial research, and clinical decision support.
What's the latency, really? Is sub-1.2s accurate?
▼
The P95 response time for the Production Matrix tier is under 1.2 seconds across our current infrastructure. Factual questions with a narrow retrieval scope are typically faster (700–900ms). Complex multi-hop queries with broad verification requirements may approach 1.8–2.2s at P99. We publish latency percentiles in our status dashboard in real time.
Is my query data used to train future models?
▼
No. VeriLife does not use customer query data for model training by default. Enterprise customers receive contractual data non-use guarantees. Free Sandbox queries may be reviewed by our team for quality assurance, which is disclosed in the Terms of Service.
